Buying proper gear part 3

These are things i haven't bought yet

  • Goggles

    • Buying goggles. The goggles should fit properly sitting on your helmet. You can get goggles that match you hear.They should not be too tight that you can't fit them over your helmet. There are different types of goggles, ones that are tinted on the outside and you can't see your eyes,and their are goggles that you can see right through and you can see your eyes. The goggles that are tinted to help with the sun just like sunglasses. The goggles that go with my helmet cost $69.95 on sale they are $55.96.

  • Elbow pads.

    • Buying elbow pads. Elbow pads should be tight to your arm and elbow. The pad should fit your arm properly they shouldn't be bigger than your elbow or smaller than your elbow. Elbow pads help when you crash they protect your arm from getting scratched and cut. Elbow pads are worth spending the money on they will save your arm from injuries. Some elbow pads do cost a lot but would you rather get your arm all cut up and scratched or spent the extra money and get more protection. Elbow pads cost anywhere from $34.95 - $134.95 and higher

  • Knee pads

    • Buying knee pads. Knee pads should be tight to your legs and knee. The pads should not be baggy or way to tight that you cant move. Knee pads help when you crash, they protect part of your leg and your knees from scratches and cuts. Knee pads are something you should look into buying before you start riding your bike, they will help you a lot if you are just starting and you will fall off a lot. Knee pads are something you really want to get I believe you would rather spend a little bit of extra money then severely hurting your leg. Knee pads cost anywhere from $39.95 - $89.95 and higher.
